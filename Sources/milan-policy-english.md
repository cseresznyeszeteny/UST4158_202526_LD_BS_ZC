## TITLE I – General Provisions

### Article 1 – Subject and Objectives

1. In consideration of the recurring exceedance of regulatory concentration limits for atmospheric particulate matter, nitrogen oxides and, more generally, the deterioration of air quality in the City of Milan, this Regulation introduces – in accordance with the principles of local autonomy – restrictions on activities that generate atmospheric emissions, with the aim of safeguarding air quality and promoting energy savings, as well as preventing environmental degradation, in compliance with Art. 3-ter, Part I of Legislative Decree 152/2006 (Consolidated Environmental Act): *"The protection of the environment and natural ecosystems and cultural heritage must be guaranteed by all public and private entities and by natural and legal persons, public or private, through adequate action informed by the principles of precaution, preventive action, correction at source of damage caused to the environment, and the 'polluter pays' principle which, pursuant to Article 174, paragraph 2, of the Treaty of the European Union, govern Community policy in environmental matters."*

2. This Regulation is approved pursuant to Article 7 of Legislative Decree No. 267/2000 and subsequent amendments, which establishes that *"in compliance with the principles set by law and the statute, municipalities and provinces shall adopt regulations in matters of their own competence (…)"*, as well as the subsequent Article 50, paragraph 7-ter, which specifies that *"in the matters referred to in paragraph 5, second period (situations of serious neglect or degradation of the territory, the environment and cultural heritage, or harm to urban decorum and liveability), municipalities may adopt regulations"*.

3. The rules contained herein comply with the general provisions of law and the relevant deliberative measures applying them, and are coordinated with the specific provisions of current municipal regulations.

---

### Article 2 – Areas of Intervention

In relation to the objectives set out in Art. 1, the Regulation intervenes in the following ways:

a. promotes the renewal of civil heating systems where fuelled by combustibles with high atmospheric emissions, in order to reduce the particulate matter they generate;
b. introduces measures to limit energy dispersion and reduce CO₂ emissions in commercial establishments, public premises and buildings open to the public;
c. promotes the renewal of power generators, where fuelled by combustibles with high atmospheric emissions, used primarily by commercial activities carried out in public areas;
d. introduces precautionary measures for the management of construction sites in the city, with the aim of reducing locally generated particulate matter;
e. establishes provisions on the prohibition of outdoor smoking and combustion;
f. promotes the spread of electric vehicle charging infrastructure across the municipal territory, in line with current traffic regulations.

### Article 4 – Combustion of Woody Biomass for Productive and Catering Activities

1. Food production and catering businesses already using woody biomass at the time this Regulation enters into force are required to submit to the Municipality of Milan, by 1 March 2021, information concerning the biomass equipment and flue system, as further defined by the relevant municipal offices.

2. In the case of new businesses or changes of ownership, food production and catering businesses intending to use woody biomass must submit the relevant information on the biomass equipment and flue system within 30 days of filing the SCIA (Certified Notice of Business Commencement).

3. In the event of replacement of the biomass equipment and/or the dust abatement system, food production and catering businesses using woody biomass are required to notify the Municipality within 30 days of the replacement, with updated information as per paragraph 1.

4. From 1 October 2022, food production and catering businesses using woody biomass are required to use biofuels of at least Class A1 (pursuant to current UNI EN ISO technical standards), certified by an accredited body. Biofuels must be stored in a manner that prevents alteration of their properties. Relevant documentation certifying biofuel supply must be retained for at least one year and made available upon request to inspection personnel.

5. In the case of new installation and/or replacement of biomass equipment and/or reconstruction of the flue system, food production and catering businesses using woody biomass are required to provide at least one combustion product sampling port, the characteristics of which will be defined by the relevant municipal offices.

## TITLE III – Productive Activities

### Article 5 – Door Closure for Commercial and Public Establishments

1. From 1 January 2022, commercial establishments, public premises and buildings open to the public in which a heating or air conditioning system of any rated thermal power is active are required to keep the conditioned space thermally isolated; accordingly, the public access points to interior spaces must not be left permanently open. Instead of ordinary doors, access devices that ensure thermal isolation (e.g. revolving doors) and air curtain devices are permitted.

2. Air curtain devices referred to in paragraph 1 must meet the following requirements:
   - must be installed in a workmanlike manner;
   - the air flow must not be heated by electric resistance;
   - must ensure that the width of the air curtain is not less than the width of the establishment's external opening;
   - must be designed to ensure an air flow velocity at floor level (determined in accordance with current technical standards) of not less than 2 m/s.

3. Establishments whose access points face shared distribution spaces that are separated from the outside and climate-controlled are exempt from the obligation in paragraph 1.

4. Air curtain devices already installed at the time this Regulation enters into force that do not meet the requirements of paragraph 2 must be adapted or decommissioned by no later than 1 June 2022.

---

### Article 6 – Commerce in Public Areas

1. From 1 January 2022, commercial activities in public areas (other than those referred to in paragraph 2) are prohibited from using power generators (electricity generators) equipped with internal combustion engines.

2. From 1 October 2022, commercial or food service activities in public areas that require food preservation, refrigeration or cooking systems are prohibited from using the following types of power generators equipped with internal combustion engines:
   - diesel-powered generators;
   - generators powered by petrol/oil mixture with a two-stroke engine.

   Relevant technical documentation proving the type of engine or fuel used must be available for inspection.

3. For vehicles equipped as mobile shops using power generators, the following rules apply:
   - if the generators are integrated into the vehicle, the obligations under paragraph 2 apply from 1 October 2028;
   - if the generators are external to the vehicle, the obligations and deadlines of paragraph 2 apply.

4. From 1 January 2022, concessionaires of commercial activities in public areas outside markets with a designated pitch (as identified in Art. 21 of the Regulation for Commerce in Public Areas of the Municipality of Milan) operating in the same location for at least four days a week are prohibited from using autonomous electricity supply systems as a substitute for connection to the electrical grid. The steps required to connect to the grid, as well as the associated costs, are the responsibility of the concessionaires.

## TITLE IV – Construction Sites

### Article 7 – Management of Construction Site Areas

1. The cleaning activities for construction site areas as defined in Art. 31 of the Municipal Regulation on *"Urban and Equivalent Waste Management and the Protection of Environmental Decorum and Hygiene"* – namely: *"carrying out cleaning by sweeping, following watering, of road stretches and public areas or areas open to the public adjacent to construction sites, where the transit of vehicles, for whatever reason, causes soiling through materials deposited by tyres or other locomotion components (tracks, etc.)"* – must be carried out with the following frequency:
   - once a day, in the case of occasional transit (fewer than ten vehicle transits per day) of construction vehicles causing soiling of roads or public areas;
   - every four hours of construction site operation, in the case of frequent transit (ten or more vehicle transits per day) of construction vehicles causing soiling.

2. In addition to the above, for construction sites with unpaved surfaces used by vehicles, the following are also required:
   - washing the wheels of vehicles leaving the construction site;
   - keeping construction site tracks wet;
   - maintaining a particularly slow transit speed for vehicles on unpaved routes within the construction site area.

3. Construction sites are also required to:
   - use vehicles with closed or covered beds, or devices to contain dust dispersion, for the movement of earth, aggregates and powdery materials;
   - store cement, lime and other powdery solid construction materials in silos and, where technically possible, transport them via closed systems;
   - screen plants that generate dust emissions (e.g. concrete mixing plants).

---

### Article 8 – Non-Road Mobile Machinery

1. The following prohibition on use applies to internal combustion engines installed on non-road mobile machinery covered by European Directives 97/68/EC and 2004/26/EC and EU Regulation 2016/1628 and amendments, used in construction and work sites:
   - from 1 October 2022, for engines of 19–560 kW in Stage I or earlier (permitted engines: category not lower than D, E, F, G);
   - from 1 October 2023, for engines of 19–560 kW in Stage II or earlier (permitted engines: category not lower than H, I, J, K);
   - from 1 October 2028, for engines (except constant-speed engines) of 37–560 kW in Stage IIIA or earlier (permitted engines: category not lower than K, L, M, N, P);
   - from 1 October 2030, for engines (except constant-speed engines) of 56–560 kW in Stage IIIB or earlier (permitted engines: category not lower than K, P, Q, R);
   - from 1 October 2039, for engines of any power rating in Stage IV or earlier.

2. Compression-ignition engines meeting Stage I, II or IIIA homologation criteria and equipped with post-treatment devices for particulate mass reduction, approved pursuant to Ministerial Decree No. 39 of 25 January 2008 or UNECE Regulation No. 132/2015, and capable of meeting the Stage IIIB PM emission limits, are exempt from the obligations in paragraph 1 until 30 September 2030.

3. The following compression-ignition engines are also exempt from paragraph 1 until 30 September 2039:
   - Stage IIIB engines of 56–560 kW equipped with UNECE-approved retrofit emission control devices capable of meeting Stage IV NOx limits or equivalent to categories Q or R for NOx;
   - Stage II or IIIA engines of 56–560 kW equipped with UNECE-approved retrofit devices capable of meeting Stage IV limits for both PM and NOx;
   - Stage II or IIIA engines of 56–560 kW equipped with both post-treatment devices for PM reduction (approved per Ministerial Decree No. 39/2008) and retrofit NOx control devices (approved per UNECE Regulation No. 132/2015) capable of meeting Stage IIIB PM limits and Stage IV NOx limits.

4. To prove compliance with paragraphs 2 and 3, the following documentation must be available for inspection: certificate of conformity/homologation of the post-treatment device, proof of installation by an authorised workshop, and proof of payment for the device.

## TITLE V – Outdoor Smoking and Combustion

### Article 9 – Outdoor Smoking

1. Without prejudice to existing legislation on smoking prohibitions, from 1 January 2021, smoking is prohibited in the following areas:
   - areas designated as public green spaces, except in isolated spots where it is possible to maintain a distance of at least 10 metres from other people;
   - equipped areas for children's play, sport or recreational activities, as governed by Art. 21, paragraph 7 of the Municipal Regulation on the Use and Protection of Public and Private Green Areas;
   - public transport stops, including taxi ranks, within a distance of 10 metres from shelters and signage infrastructure;
   - cemetery areas;
   - dog areas;
   - sports facilities of any type, including public areas (e.g. spectator stands).

2. From 1 January 2025, the smoking ban is extended to all public areas or areas for public use, including road areas, except in isolated spots where it is possible to maintain a distance of at least 10 metres from other people.

## TITLE VII – Controls and Sanctions

### Article 12 – Supervisory Activities

The Municipal Administration shall exercise its supervisory functions to ensure compliance with this Regulation within the territory of the Municipality of Milan. This activity is entrusted to the Local Police (Polizia Locale).

---

### Article 13 – Sanctions

Unless the act constitutes a criminal offence, violations of the provisions of this Regulation shall be subject to the administrative sanctions listed in Annex A (which forms an integral part of this Regulation), applied in the forms and manner provided by Law No. 689 of 24 November 1981, and, where not covered by that table, the provisions of Art. 7-bis of Legislative Decree No. 267 of 18 August 2000.

---

## TITLE VIII – Transitional and Final Provisions

### Article 14 – Entry into Force

This Regulation enters into force on the thirtieth day following the date of enforceability of the Municipal Council resolution approving it.

---

### Article 15 – Repeal of Previous Rules and Reference to Other Provisions

Unless otherwise provided, the rules of this Regulation shall prevail over other regulatory rules incompatible with it.

---

### Article 16 – Regulation Updates

Subject to any necessary adaptation of this Regulation due to new national or regional legislation, amendments to this Regulation are the responsibility of the Municipal Council.
