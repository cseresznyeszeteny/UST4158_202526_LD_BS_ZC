# Bibliography: Milan's Outdoor Smoking Ban and Tobacco Control in Italy

---

## Research Articles

Asta, F., V. Minardi, B. Contoli, V. Possenti, and M. Masocco. "New Tobacco Products and Smoking Prevalence in Italy: A Double-Intervention Time Series Analysis." *European Journal of Public Health* 35, Supplement 4 (October 2025): ckaf161.886. https://doi.org/10.1093/eurpub/ckaf161.886. https://www.researchgate.net/publication/396944149_New_tobacco_products_and_smoking_prevalence_in_Italy_a_double-intervention_time_series_analysis.

> Using PASSI surveillance system data from over 530,000 interviews conducted between 2008 and 2023, this interrupted time series analysis finds that the introduction of electronic cigarettes and heated tobacco products in Italy did not lead to a decrease in overall tobacco and nicotine use among adults, suggesting that novel devices failed to curb conventional smoking prevalence.

---

Gallus, S., P. Zuccaro, P. Colombo, G. Apolone, R. Pacifici, S. Garattini, and C. La Vecchia. "Effects of New Smoking Regulations in Italy." *Annals of Oncology* 17, no. 2 (February 2006): 346–347. https://doi.org/10.1093/annonc/mdj070. https://www.researchgate.net/publication/6154209_Effects_of_new_smoking_regulations_in_Italy.

> Drawing on a nationally representative survey of 3,114 Italians conducted shortly after the January 2005 indoor smoking ban, this study reports that public support for smoke-free policies increased once the regulations were in force, and that the ban was associated with approximately an 8% short-term reduction in cigarette consumption, with especially marked decreases among young smokers aged 15–24.

---

La Vecchia, Carlo, Angela D. Alicandro, Angelita L. Annunziata, and Simone Milanesi. "Milan, Italy: New Smoking Regulation in Public Places, Effective January 2025." *Journal of Health Inequalities* 11, no. 1 (2025): 1–3. https://www.researchgate.net/publication/393227950_Milan_Italy_new_smoking_regulation_in_public_places_effective_January_2025.

> This special article contextualises Milan's January 2025 total outdoor smoking ban within Italy's broader tobacco control history, noting that no major national regulation had been adopted since the 2005 Sirchia Law and that Italy's Tobacco Control Scale ranking dropped from 8th to 18th place in Europe between 2006 and 2021; the authors argue the Milan ordinance offers a replicable model for other Italian and European cities.

---

Possenti, Irene, Marco Scala, Anna Odone, Luc J.M. Smits, Silvano Gallus, and Alessandra Lugo. "Prevalence of Cigarette Smoking and Secondhand Smoke Exposure in Italy in 2024." *Tumori Journal* (2025). https://doi.org/10.1177/03008916251378261. https://www.researchgate.net/publication/396749096_Prevalence_of_cigarette_smoking_and_secondhand_smoke_exposure_in_Italy_in_2024.

> Based on a nationally representative face-to-face survey of 3,125 Italians aged 15 and older conducted in 2024, this cross-sectional study finds that 26.6% of Italian adults were current conventional cigarette smokers — reversing the long-term declining trend — and that 16.3% of non-smokers reported daily indoor exposure to secondhand smoke, with the authors calling for updated tobacco control policies and stricter regulation of novel nicotine products.

## News Articles

Anadolu Agency. "Milan to Ban Smoking in Public Places Starting 2025." *Anadolu Agency*, December 2024. https://www.aa.com.tr/en/europe/milan-to-ban-smoking-in-public-places-starting-2025/3427386.

> This report announces Milan's forthcoming January 2025 outdoor smoking ban, outlining the extension of restrictions to all public spaces with fines between €40 and €240, while also noting that tobacco producers and retailers were planning to contest the measure in regional courts on jurisdictional grounds.

---

Anadolu Agency. "Smoke-Free Milan? New Ban Ignites Debate on Liberties." *Anadolu Agency*, January 2025. https://www.aa.com.tr/en/europe/smoke-free-milan-new-ban-ignites-debate-on-liberties/3453696.

> This article captures the public debate surrounding Milan's new outdoor smoking ban, presenting reactions from both supporters — including oncologist Silvio Garattini, who cited passive smoking deaths and cigarette butt pollution — and critics from the retail sector, while noting a lack of public signage and concerns about enforcement.

---

Euronews. "Milan Cracks Down on Outdoor Smoking in Toughest Ban to Date." *Euronews*, January 1, 2025. https://www.euronews.com/health/2025/01/01/milan-cracks-down-on-outdoor-smoking-in-toughest-ban-to-date.

> This news piece reports the entry into force of Milan's citywide outdoor smoking ban on January 1, 2025, describing the ban as the strictest in a series of progressive measures launched in 2021 to improve air quality, with fines of €40–€240 for violations and a 10-metre exception for isolated areas.

---

*Hospitality Inside*. "Milan Struggles with Outdoor Smoking Ban." *Hospitality Inside*, 2025. https://hospitalityinside.com/en/Milan-struggles-with-outdoor-smoking-ban.

> This trade publication piece examines the practical challenges facing the hospitality industry under Milan's outdoor smoking ban, highlighting concerns about enforcement responsibility, unclear rules for outdoor dining terraces, and criticism from business associations who describe the ordinance as difficult to implement.

---

*Italia Absolutely*. "Outdoor Smoking Ban Brings Fresh Air to Italy's Fashion Capital." *Italia Absolutely*, 2025. https://italiabsolutely.com/editorial/outdoor-smoking-ban-brings-fresh-air-to-italy-s-fashion-capital.

> This editorial frames Milan's outdoor smoking ban as a positive step for public health and environmental quality, positioning the city as a leader in smoke-free urban policy and exploring what the change means for residents, visitors, and Italy's broader tobacco control landscape.

---

*Italian American Herald*. "Milan's Rules on Smoking Extend to Venues at 2026 Winter Olympics." *Italian American Herald*, January 19, 2026. https://italianamericanherald.com/milans-rules-on-smoking-extend-to-venues-at-2026-winter-olympics/.

> This article examines how Milan's 2025 outdoor smoking ban applies to venues hosting the Milan–Cortina 2026 Winter Olympics, noting limited enforcement since the ban's introduction, the "symbolic" characterisation of the ordinance by business federation president Lino Stoppani, and the practical implications for the thousands of international athletes and spectators attending the Games.

*The New York Times*. "Milan's Outdoor Smoking Ban." *The New York Times*, February 5, 2025. https://www.nytimes.com/2025/02/05/world/europe/milan-outdoor-smoking-ban-italy.html.

> This feature takes stock of Milan's outdoor smoking ban one month after its launch, examining whether the regulation is being observed on city streets, how residents and businesses are adapting, and what the measure signals about Italy's evolving approach to public health policy.

---

*Tobacco Reporter*. "Milan Bans Outdoor Smoking." *Tobacco Reporter*, January 2, 2025. https://tobaccoreporter.com/2025/01/02/milan-bans-outdoor-smoking/.

> This brief industry news item reports the effective date and scope of Milan's outdoor smoking ban, noting that it covers all public spaces with a 10-metre isolation exception and that the city ranks among Europe's most polluted, framing the regulation within an air quality improvement rationale.

---

*Unione Sarda*. "Milan: From January 1st, Absolute Ban on Smoking Outdoors." *Unione Sarda*, January 2025. https://www.unionesarda.it/en/italy/milan-from-january-1st-absolute-ban-on-smoking-outdoors-exi4omlk.

> This Italian news outlet reports the details of Milan's new outdoor smoking ordinance, explaining that the ban covers all public or publicly accessible outdoor areas including streets and squares, with fines ranging from €40 to €250, while electronic cigarettes remain unaffected by the regulation.

---

*Wanted in Milan*. "Milan Ban: Smoking in Outdoor Public Places." *Wanted in Milan*, 2025. https://www.wantedinmilan.com/news/milan-ban-smoking-outdoor-public-places.html.

> Targeted at expatriates and visitors, this article provides a practical overview of Milan's outdoor smoking ban, explaining where the rules apply, what exceptions exist, what fines smokers risk, and how the ordinance fits into the city's wider Air and Climate Plan adopted in 2020.
