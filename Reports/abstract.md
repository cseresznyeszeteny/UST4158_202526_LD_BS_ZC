## SMOKING BAN IN MILAN: POLICY REVIEW AND SHORT-TERM EFFECTS

As of January 2nd, 2025, Milan, The Moral Capital of Italy, banned outdoor smoking in all public areas making an exception for secluded areas that are at least 10 meters away from the public. Those who do not adhere to the rules are fined from €40–€240. This was a response to the city's 2020 air quality review that scored poorly, which prompted it to be forbidden to smoke at bus stops, playgrounds, sporting areas, and parks beginning from 2021. The aim of this project is to review existing literature on smoking bans, review gaps in the implemented policy, analyse daily air pollution data between 2024 and 2026 and explore smoking habits survey data in Milan.
